# Maintenance Sources

OpenDash's bundled spare-parts schedule for the default Himalayan 450 is based on the official **New Himalayan 450 Owner's Manual (UK English)**, section **Periodical Maintenance**, printed pages 122-127 (PDF pages 124-129).

The application uses the recurring schedule after the initial 500 km service:

| Item | App action | Official recurring schedule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Engine oil | Replace | Every 10,000 km or 12 months; check level every 1,000 km |
| Engine oil filter | Replace | Every 10,000 km or 12 months |
| Air filter element | Replace | Every 10,000 km or 12 months; more often in dusty conditions |
| Front and rear brake pads | Inspect | Every 10,000 km or 12 months; replace if necessary |
| Front and rear tyre wear | Inspect | Every 10,000 km or 12 months; replace if necessary |
| Rear wheel drive chain | Maintain | Clean, lubricate, and adjust every 500 km or earlier, and after wet, dusty, or muddy riding |

The manual states that scheduled work is due **whichever is earlier** between distance and time, and that intervals should be shortened for severe or dusty use. OpenDash therefore evaluates both kilometres and calendar time for entries with a time interval.

The official manual does not specify fixed replacement distances for brake pads, tyres, or the chain and sprocket set. OpenDash must not present inspection or maintenance intervals as guaranteed replacement-life estimates.

Schedules for user-added vehicles are intentionally not inferred from this Himalayan manual. Riders should enter the intervals from the official manual for their exact model and market.
